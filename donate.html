Help By Donation


NEW LIFE RESIDENTIAL CENTER'S CURRENT CHALLENGES:

Budget Cuts

With the continual decline in financial assistance from the Department of Social Services to women in transition, the cost to support and maintain room and board , programs and services is a challenging endeavor.

Transitional Housing

Governmental agencies are requiring earlier releases from residential centers which are forcing women to transition to independent living quarters prematurely and without continued support, programs and services.

NEW LIFE RESIDENTIAL CENTER'S RESPONSE TO THESE CURRENT CHALLENGES:

INCREASE FUND RAISING ACTIVITIES THROUGH MORE CONCENTRATED CAMPAIGNS, GRANT WRITING INITIATIVES, CORPORATE SPONSORSHIP AND PRIVATE INDIVIDUAL APPEALS in order to provide additional monies to support and maintain services and programs for our residents.

PURCHASE ADDITIONAL RESIDENTIAL PROPERTIES to provide additional living quarters for the ever-growing population of women in transition and rehab properties for post-program women seeking rental properties they can afford.

MARKETING, MEDIA & NETWORKING CAMPAIGNS strategically designed to promote community awareness and volunteer support of the New Life Residential Center.

How each $1.00 you contribute significantly IMPACTS the women of the New Life Residential Center Inc.

  • $1: (1) Household item from the local Dollar Stores needed when a woman transitions into her new apartment after she leaves New Life Residential Center.

  • $5: A NFTA Metro Day Pass needed to provide transportation for a woman to and from doctor appointments, classes at school and job interviews.

  • $352: The amount of money each woman is awarded from the Department of Social Services for rent for her own apartment after she leaves New Life Residential Center.

  • $664: The amount the Department of Social Services awards each woman for room and board at New Life Residential Center. The cost is about $900.

Every dollar counts. Every donation big or small will make a significant impact on the lives of the women in transition in the New Life Residential Center Inc.
